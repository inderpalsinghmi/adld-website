Research Roadmap

Our agile strategic priorities through 2027 — clinical trials, novel drug development, and disease tracking running in parallel.

At the ADLD Center, we are committed to accelerating progress toward a cure for Autosomal Dominant Leukodystrophy. Our agile roadmap outlines our strategic priorities through 2027, focusing on parallel streams of clinical trials, novel drug development, and comprehensive disease tracking. Every initiative is designed to build on the others — creating momentum that compounds.

T1

ASO Clinical Trial Program

Active — First-in-Human Trial

Antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs) are synthetic molecules designed to silence the overexpressed LMNB1 gene — the root cause of ADLD. The ADLD Center's first-in-human n=1 trial marks a historic milestone: the first therapeutic intervention ever attempted for this disease.

Completed

Preclinical Validation

ASO efficacy confirmed in mouse models

Underway

n=1 Compassionate Use Trial

First-in-human intrathecal ASO treatment

Next

IND Filing & Cohort Expansion

Scale to multi-patient Phase I/II trial

T2

Drug Repurposing

In Progress

While ASO development advances, the ADLD Center is simultaneously screening FDA-approved compounds for myelin-protective and neuroprotective effects in ADLD models. Drug repurposing offers the fastest possible path to a treatment patients can access today.

Completed

Candidate Identification

Shortlist of FDA-approved compounds

Underway

Preclinical Screening

Testing in LMNB1 overexpression models

Planned

Clinical Translation

Compassionate use or off-label pathway

T3

Natural History Study

Now Enrolling

A longitudinal study of ADLD patients worldwide — tracking disease progression, symptoms, and imaging over time. This dataset will serve as a synthetic control arm for future clinical trials, dramatically reducing the number of patients needed in placebo groups and accelerating regulatory approval.

Live

Global Patient Registry

Enrolling patients across multiple countries

Underway

Longitudinal Data Collection

MRI, functional assessments, biosamples

Goal

Synthetic Control Arm

FDA-accepted dataset for trial design

T4

Biomarker Development

In Progress

Without reliable biomarkers, it's impossible to measure whether a treatment is working. The ADLD Center is working to identify blood and CSF biomarkers that reflect disease activity in real time — enabling objective endpoints for clinical trials and faster regulatory decisions.

Underway

Candidate Biomarker Panel

NfL, GFAP, and myelin-related proteins

Underway

NHS Integration

Biosamples collected via Natural History Study

Planned

Validated Trial Endpoints
